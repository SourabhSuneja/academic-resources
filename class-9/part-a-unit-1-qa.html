Communication Skills - Q&A

Short Answer Type Questions

1. What is encoding in communication?

Encoding means turning thoughts into words, signs, or actions before sending a message.

2. From where is the term communication derived?

The word "communication" comes from the Latin word "communis" which means "to share".

3. How does the environment affect perspective in communication?

Environment can change how we feel and think, which affects how we understand messages.

4. What is the meaning of coherent in communication?

Coherent means the message is clear, logical, and easy to follow.

5. What is oral communication?

Oral communication is when we talk or speak to others using words.

6. Name the type of sentences that use question marks.

They are called interrogative sentences.

7. What are indefinite articles? Give examples.

Indefinite articles are "a" and "an". Example: a pen, an apple.

Long Answer Type Questions

1. What is written communication? Write any four advantages of it.

Written communication is sharing messages through writing like letters or emails.

  • Keeps record
  • Can be read again
  • Good for official use
  • Less chance of forgetting

2. Discuss any two types of non-verbal communication.

  • Facial Expressions: Showing feelings through face (like smiling).
  • Gestures: Hand or body movements to express something.

4. What are adjectives? Give an example.

Adjectives are words that describe a noun. Example: red apple, tall boy.

5. What is apostrophe (')? Where is it used?

An apostrophe (') shows possession or missing letters.

  • Possession: Rahul's book
  • Missing letters: can't (cannot)
