Communication Skills - Notes

1. What is Communication?

Communication is the process of sharing ideas, thoughts, and information between people.

2. Importance of Communication

  • Helps in expressing thoughts and emotions.
  • Builds relationships and teamwork.
  • Avoids misunderstandings.
  • Essential for learning and career success.

3. Elements of Communication

  • Sender: The person who shares the message.
  • Message: The information being shared.
  • Encoding: Converting thoughts into words, signs, or gestures.
  • Channel: The medium (text, email etc) through which messages travel.
  • Receiver: The person who gets the message.
  • Decoding: Understanding the message.
  • Feedback: The response given by the receiver.

4. Perspective and Its Impact on Communication

Perspective is how we see and understand things based on our past experiences and beliefs.

Example: A teacher correcting a student may be seen as guidance by one student but as criticism by another.

5. Prejudice and Its Effect on Communication

Prejudice is judging others without knowing them properly. It creates barriers in communication.

Example: Thinking someone is rude just because of their appearance can lead to misunderstanding.

6. Cultural Differences in Communication

Different cultures interpret the same thing differently.

Example: A thumbs-up gesture means "good" in many countries but is offensive in some cultures.

7. Types of Communication

  • Verbal: Spoken or written words.
  • Non-Verbal: Body language, gestures, facial expressions.
  • Visual: Signs, symbols, charts, images.

8. Types of Verbal Communication

  • Oral Communication: Talking, speeches, conversations.
  • Written Communication: Emails, letters, books, reports.

9. Advantages & Disadvantages of Oral and Written Communication

Type Advantages Disadvantages
Oral Quick, direct, personal Can be misunderstood, no record
Written Clear, keeps records, formal Takes time, no immediate feedback

10. The 7 Cs of Communication

The 7 Cs of Communication ensure that messages are clear, concise, and effective. These are:

  • 1. Clarity: The message should be clear and easy to understand.
  • 2. Conciseness: Keep the message short and to the point.
  • 3. Correctness: Use accurate facts, grammar, and language.
  • 4. Completeness: Provide all necessary details so the receiver understands fully.
  • 5. Concreteness: Use specific details and facts to make the message strong and convincing.
  • 6. Courtesy: Be polite, respectful, and considerate in communication.
