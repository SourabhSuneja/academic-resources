Self-Management Skills - Q&A

Short Answer Type Questions

1. What is self-control? Give an example.

Self-control means managing your actions, emotions, and thoughts in different situations. For example, not getting angry even if someone says something rude.

2. What are organisational skills?

Organisational skills help you plan, arrange, and finish your work properly. They help in using time and resources well.

3. Lack of self-confidence often lands a person in unwanted situations. Comment.

People with low confidence may follow others blindly, make wrong choices, or not speak up when needed. This can lead to problems.

4. What is the advantage of having a good hygiene?

Good hygiene keeps you clean and healthy. It also creates a good impression on others and prevents illness.

5. What is the role of experience in building up self-confidence?

When we succeed in something, it makes us believe in ourselves. More positive experiences help build strong confidence.

Long Answer Type Questions

1. List any four positive results of self-management.

  • Helps to achieve goals
  • Reduces stress
  • Improves focus and discipline
  • Makes you more responsible

2. Write the steps to identify your strengths.

  • Think about what you enjoy doing
  • Check what others praise you for
  • Try different activities
  • Notice where you perform better than others

3. Why is it important to know yourself? Write some important points that help you in the process of knowing yourself.

Knowing yourself helps you make better decisions, set the right goals, and be confident. It helps you grow.

  • Think about your likes and dislikes
  • Notice your habits and thoughts
  • Understand your strengths and weaknesses
  • Ask for feedback from people you trust

4. How do social factors help in building self-confidence?

Support from family, friends, and teachers helps us feel better about ourselves. Positive feedback and appreciation also boost confidence.

5. What is the difference between attitudes of people with low and high self-confidence?

  • Low confidence: Afraid to try new things, need others' approval, fear failure
  • High confidence: Believe in themselves, try challenges, accept mistakes and learn
