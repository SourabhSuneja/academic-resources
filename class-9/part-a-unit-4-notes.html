Entrepreneurial Skills - I Notes

1. Definition of Business

Business refers to any activity carried out to produce and sell goods or services in order to earn profit and satisfy customer needs.

2. Types of Business

  • Manufacturing: Produces goods using raw materials. Example: Car factory.
  • Service: Provides services instead of physical goods. Example: Hospitals, Schools.
  • Merchandising: Buys products from suppliers and sells them to customers. Example: Retail shops.
  • Hybrid: Combination of two or more types, such as a restaurant (manufactures + serves).

3. Forms of Business Ownership

Form Features Advantages Disadvantages
Sole Proprietorship Owned and managed by one person. Easy to start, owner keeps all profit, full control. Unlimited liability, limited resources.
Partnership Owned by two or more people. More capital, shared responsibility. Conflicts may arise, unlimited liability.
Company/Corporate A legal entity separate from owners. Limited liability, large capital, perpetual existence. Complex formation, heavy regulations.
Limited Liability Company (LLC) Mix of partnership and company. Limited liability, flexible structure. Varies by law, limited growth potential.

4. Steps of Starting a Business

  • Identify a business idea.
  • Do market research.
  • Prepare a business plan.
  • Arrange finance.
  • Register the business legally.
  • Set up operations and hire staff.
  • Promote and launch the business.

5. Who is an Entrepreneur?

An entrepreneur is a person who identifies opportunities, takes risks, and builds a business to earn profit and contribute to society.

6. What is Entrepreneurship?

Entrepreneurship is the process of starting, organizing, and managing a business venture with the aim of making profit while taking risks.

7. Process of Entrepreneurship Development

  • Idea generation
  • Feasibility study
  • Business plan preparation
  • Resource mobilization
  • Business launch
  • Growth and expansion

8. Characteristics of an Entrepreneur

  • Risk-taking capability
  • Innovative and creative
  • Visionary with leadership qualities
  • Open-minded and flexible
  • Confident and determined
  • Hard-working and persistent

9. Role of an Entrepreneur

  • Economic development: Creates jobs and wealth.
  • Social development: Improves lifestyle and services.
  • Innovation: Brings new products and ideas.

10. Core Skills of a Successful Entrepreneur

  • Problem-solving skills
  • Communication and networking
  • Decision-making ability
  • Time management
  • Financial literacy
  • Adaptability

11. Challenges of Entrepreneurship

  • Uncertain income and financial risks
  • High competition
  • Stress and workload
  • Lack of support and resources

12. Rewards of Entrepreneurship

  • Master of own time
  • Lives passion
  • Be own boss
  • Makes profit for self
  • Creates impact on society

13. Businessman vs Entrepreneur

Businessman Entrepreneur
Works on existing ideas. Creates new ideas and innovations.
Focuses mainly on profit. Focuses on solving problems and growth.
Follows traditional methods. Takes risks and experiments.
Competes with others. Creates new markets.
Runs the business for stability. Builds the business for impact and change.
