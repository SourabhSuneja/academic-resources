Entrepreneurship Skills - Q&A

Application-based Questions

1. Susan is an MBA graduate in Sales and Marketing and now wants to start her own business. Her father is a cab driver and she neither has money to invest in her business nor wants to take any loan. She wants to go into a service business. Can you suggest some service businesses where with less investment she can start her own business?

Yes, Susan can start businesses that require skills rather than money. Examples include:

  • Digital marketing services
  • Event planning and coordination
  • Online tutoring or coaching
  • Content writing and freelancing
  • Social media management

2. Amit has opened his own stationery shop and in the initial one year he managed to earn profit.

a. What type of ownership of the business he possesses? Write its name.

He owns a Sole Proprietorship.

b. Give its important features.

  • Owned and controlled by one person
  • Easy to set up
  • Owner keeps full profit
  • Owner bears all risks

c. What are the social objectives of his business?

  • Providing quality stationery to students and offices
  • Creating employment opportunities
  • Contributing to local community needs

3. Your cousin is planning to be an entrepreneur but he is in a dilemma whether to take risk or not. How can you help him convince to go ahead in living his dream of a successful entrepreneur?

I would explain that every successful entrepreneur has to take risks. Risks bring challenges but also opportunities. Proper planning, hard work, and learning from mistakes reduce risks. If he never takes risks, he may miss his chance of success. Believing in himself is the first step toward becoming a successful entrepreneur.

Short Answer Type Questions

1. Who is a businessman? Give a few examples.

A businessman is a person engaged in commercial or trade activities to earn profit. Examples: shopkeeper, wholesaler, manufacturer.

2. What do you mean by “Feedback in the Long Run”?

It means the continuous response and results an entrepreneur receives after operating a business for a long time, which helps in improving performance.

3. What is a Corporation? Name a few.

A corporation is a large business owned by shareholders and managed by a board of directors. Examples: Reliance Industries, Infosys, Tata Motors.

4. Give two advantages of Limited Liability Company.

  • Owners’ personal assets are safe
  • Easy to raise capital from investors

5. Define an Asset. Give examples.

An asset is anything of value owned by a business. Examples: land, cash, equipment, buildings.

6. Differentiate between an entrepreneurship and an enterprise.

  • Entrepreneurship: The process of starting and managing a business
  • Enterprise: The actual business organization started

7. What do you mean by perception?

Perception is the way a person understands or interprets things based on his/her experiences and beliefs.

8. Why are “Visionary and Leadership qualities” important?

They help entrepreneurs guide their team, set future goals, motivate others, and take the business in the right direction.

9. What do you think is the biggest challenge of an entrepreneur?

The biggest challenge is taking risks and facing uncertainty while ensuring business survival and growth.

10. What is the market status of an entrepreneur?

It shows the entrepreneur’s position in the market compared to competitors, including demand for products and brand reputation.

Long Answer Type Questions

1. Differentiate between a Partnership and a Corporation.

  • Partnership: Owned by 2 or more people, easy to form, profits and losses shared, partners have unlimited liability.
  • Corporation: Owned by shareholders, managed by board of directors, separate legal identity, limited liability of shareholders.

2. Give advantages and disadvantages of Sole Proprietorship.

  • Advantages: Easy to start, full control, owner keeps all profits.
  • Disadvantages: Unlimited liability, limited resources, business ends if owner dies.

3. Give three important features of a business.

  • Production or purchase of goods and services
  • Sale or exchange for money
  • Continuous process with the aim of earning profit
