The environment includes everything around us such as air, water, land, plants, animals, and human-made things that support life.

2. Ecosystem

An ecosystem is a community of living organisms (plants, animals, humans) interacting with non-living components (air, water, soil).

Example: Forest, pond, desert, and grassland ecosystems.

3. Relationship between Society and Environment

Society and environment are closely connected and depend on each other.

  • Humans use natural resources for food, shelter, and development.
  • Human activities affect nature positively or negatively.
  • A healthy environment leads to a healthy society.

4. Natural Resources

Natural resources are materials provided by nature that humans use.

  • Renewable: Sunlight, wind, water, forests
  • Non-renewable: Coal, petroleum, natural gas

5. Conservation of Resources

Conservation means careful use and protection of natural resources to prevent their depletion.

  • Using resources wisely
  • Saving water and electricity
  • Protecting forests and wildlife

6. The 3Rs

The 3Rs help reduce waste and protect the environment.

  • Reduce: Minimize the use of resources
  • Reuse: Use items again instead of throwing them away
  • Recycle: Convert waste into useful products

7. Factors Causing Environmental Imbalance

  • Deforestation
  • Pollution (air, water, soil)
  • Overpopulation
  • Overuse of natural resources
  • Climate change

8. Meaning of Green Skills

Green Skills are the knowledge, abilities, and attitudes needed to support sustainable development and protect the environment.

Example: Waste management, renewable energy skills, water conservation.

9. Green Economy

A Green Economy is an economy that promotes environmental protection while ensuring economic growth and social well-being.

  • Uses renewable energy
  • Creates eco-friendly jobs
  • Reduces pollution and waste
  • Encourages sustainable living
