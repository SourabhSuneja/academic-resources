Arranging and Collecting Data - Q&A

Short Answer Type Questions

1. Why data needs to be collected?

Data is collected to analyze, understand, and make decisions based on facts instead of guesses.

2. What is the impact of improper data collection?

Improper data collection can lead to wrong conclusions, wasted resources, and poor decision-making.

3. How does data collection software work?

Data collection software works by gathering, storing, and organizing information automatically through forms, sensors, or online tools.

4. My book on "Data Science" got 4.5 star rating? Which type of variable is this?

This is an ordinal variable because ratings show order (1 star is less than 4.5 stars).

5. What is the amount of data in bytes over which a data set is called big data?

A dataset is called Big Data when it is too large (in terabytes or petabytes) to be handled by traditional software tools.

6. What are the five V's of Big Data?

The five V’s are: Volume, Velocity, Variety, Veracity, and Value.

Long Answer Type Questions

1. Distinguish between three types of data variables.

  • Nominal: Categories without order (e.g., colors: red, blue, green).
  • Ordinal: Categories with order (e.g., star ratings: 1 star, 2 stars, 5 stars).
  • Interval/Ratio: Numerical data with meaningful values (e.g., height, weight, temperature).

2. What are the two data collection methods? Distinguish between them.

  • Primary Data: Collected first-hand through surveys, interviews, or experiments. It is original and reliable but costly.
  • Secondary Data: Collected from already available books, articles, or reports. It is easy and cheap but may not be fully accurate.

3. What are the different methodologies used for data collection and analysis? Describe any one of them in detail.

  • Interviews
  • Surveys
  • Questionnaires
  • Case Studies
  • Focus Group Discussions
  • Smart Gadgets & Devices

Example - Surveys: A survey collects data by asking structured questions to a large group of people. It is quick, cost-effective, and helps in studying patterns.

4. What are the advantages of focus group discussion?

  • Provides in-depth opinions from participants.
  • Encourages group interaction and idea sharing.
  • Helps in understanding consumer behavior.
  • Useful in testing new products or ideas.

5. How does social media data affect human life?

Social media data impacts life by shaping opinions, advertisements, political campaigns, and even personal decisions. It can help connect people but also spread misinformation.

6. What are the techniques used in interpretation and analysis of consumer data?

  • Data mining: Finding hidden patterns.
  • Statistical analysis: Using averages and trends.
  • Predictive analysis: Guessing future behavior.
  • Visualization: Using charts and graphs to explain data.

7. What are the three distinct ways to find relationship among data?

  • Correlation: Shows relationship between two variables (e.g. Is there any relationship between the number of hours you study and your exam scores?)
  • Regression: Predicts values using relationships. (e.g. if I score 7 marks by studying for 1 hour, how much will I score if I study for 2 hours?)
  • Cross-tabulation: Compares variables in tables. (e.g. comparing prices of one company's products with another company's)

8. What are multivariate data conditions utilised for in research?

Multivariate data conditions are used when more than two variables are studied together. They help researchers understand complex relationships, patterns, and predictions in fields like healthcare, marketing, and education.
For example: If a reasearcher wants to find out what influences weight gain or loss, he will look at multiple variables such as, one's diet, exercise schedule, hours of sleep etc. Studying just one variable will not give a true picture because weight is influenced by many variables.

Extra Questions

1. How does NASA use data science?

NASA uses data science to study space missions, predict weather, monitor satellites, and make safe decisions for astronauts.

2. How does Sourabh Sir's marks management app use anomaly detection to catch data entry errors?

The app compares a student’s new score with past scores and scores in other subjects. If the new score looks very different, it is flagged as unexpected so the teacher can check it again.

3. How do F1 cars use sensors and data science? How does it help?

F1 cars have hundreds of sensors that track speed, tire temperature, fuel, and engine health. This data helps teams improve performance, plan pit-stops, and ensure safety.

4. Give an example of a classification algorithm that asks repeated Yes/No questions to solve a real-life problem.

A Decision Tree works like this. Example: To decide if you can play outside – it asks Is it raining? (Yes/No), then Is it too hot? (Yes/No), and so on until a final decision is made.
