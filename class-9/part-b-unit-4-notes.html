Ethics in Data Science - Notes

1. Data Ethics – Definition

Data Ethics refers to the moral principles and values that guide how data is collected, stored, analysed, shared, and used.

It ensures that data is used in a fair, responsible, and respectful manner.

2. Explanation of Data Ethics

In data science, large amounts of data about people are analysed. Data ethics helps ensure that:

  • People’s privacy is respected.
  • Data is not misused or manipulated.
  • Decisions made using data are fair and unbiased.

3. Need for Data Ethics

Data ethics is important because improper use of data can cause harm.

  • Protects personal and sensitive information.
  • Prevents misuse of data for fraud or discrimination.
  • Builds trust between users and organisations.
  • Ensures responsible use of AI and data-driven systems.

4. Ethical Guidelines around Data Analysis

Ethical guidelines provide rules and best practices for analysing data responsibly.

  • Privacy: Do not expose personal data.
  • Consent: Use data only with proper permission.
  • Transparency: Be clear about how data is used.
  • Fairness: Avoid bias in data and algorithms.
  • Accuracy: Use correct and reliable data.

5. Goals of Ethical Guidelines

  • To protect individuals and society.
  • To ensure fairness in data-driven decisions.
  • To promote trust in data science and AI systems.
  • To encourage responsible innovation.

6. Data Governance Framework

A Data Governance Framework is a structured system of rules, roles, and processes used to manage data properly within an organisation.

It defines who can use data, how data is handled, and how data quality is maintained.

7. Key Elements of Data Governance

  • Data policies and standards.
  • Roles and responsibilities.
  • Data quality management.
  • Data security and privacy controls.

8. Goals of Data Governance

  • To ensure data accuracy and consistency.
  • To protect data from unauthorised access.
  • To improve decision-making using reliable data.
  • To comply with laws and regulations.

9. Real-Life Example

A school collects student performance data. Ethical data practices ensure that:

  • Only teachers can access student data.
  • Data is used to help students improve, not to label them.
  • Parents’ and students’ privacy is respected.

10. Conclusion

Ethics in Data Science ensures that data is used for good purposes while protecting people’s rights. Ethical guidelines and data governance help organisations use data responsibly, safely, and fairly.
